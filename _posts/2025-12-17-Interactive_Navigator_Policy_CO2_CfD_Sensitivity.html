This interactive policy sensitivity navigator explores how carbon constraints and Contract-for-Difference (CfD) support mechanisms influence hydrogen deployment in the Great Britain (GB) power system by 2050. Developed as part of the HI-ACT project on accelerated energy transition, the tool enables dynamic assessment of hydrogen integration pathways under varying policy and economic conditions.

The navigator allows users to examine a wide range of CO₂ emission limits (0–500) and CfD support levels (0–250), and to assess their combined impact on system costs, emissions, and investment outcomes. Multiple hydrogen supply routes—including Grey H₂, Blue H₂, and Green H₂—can be interactively enabled or disabled to reflect alternative decarbonization strategies.

Users can further explore the sensitivity of results across high and low investment assumptions and feedstock price scenarios, making the tool particularly useful for policy stress-testing and comparative analysis.

Key Features

  • Interactive exploration of CO₂ emission caps and CfD support levels
  • Toggleable hydrogen production pathways (Grey, Blue, and Green H₂)
  • High/low investment and feedstock price sensitivity analysis
  • Multiple performance metrics, including:
    • Total system emissions
    • Green hydrogen penetration
    • Marginal and average system costs
    • Net Present Value (NPV)
    • Marginal abatement cost
  • Heatmap-based visualizations and policy navigators for intuitive comparison

Policy Insights

By combining carbon constraints with targeted financial support mechanisms, the navigator highlights trade-offs between cost efficiency and decarbonization ambition. It enables policymakers, system planners, and researchers to identify policy combinations that deliver meaningful emissions reductions while maintaining economic viability.

The tool is particularly useful for evaluating combined policy scores, offering a structured way to compare competing policy packages and understand how hydrogen technologies respond to evolving regulatory environments.

HI-ACT (Hydrogen Integration for Accelerated Energy Transitions) is a UK research project funded by the Engineering and Physical Sciences Research Council (EPSRC) under Grant No. EP/X038823/2. The project focuses on exploring hydrogen integration pathways to support Great Britain’s transition toward a resilient and net-zero multi-vector energy system.

The transition toward low-carbon energy systems requires coordinated planning across electricity, gas, hydrogen, heating, and transportation infrastructures. Within the HI-ACT project, our research focused on developing optimisation, planning, and policy-analysis frameworks to evaluate hydrogen integration strategies in Great Britain’s energy system.

The work combined long-term strategic planning, short-term operational optimisation, socio-technical analysis, and game-theoretic modelling to investigate how hydrogen can enhance flexibility, reduce emissions, and support renewable energy integration across multiple sectors.

Throughout the project, we developed integrated multi-vector energy system models using Python-based frameworks including pandapower, pandapipes, and Pyomo. The research explored hydrogen production, storage, blending, transportation, and hydrogen-to-power technologies under different future decarbonisation scenarios.

Research Themes and Contributions

  • Residential Heating Decarbonisation: Investigated socio-technical pathways for reducing emissions from residential heating systems in the North of Tyne region through social interventions, heat pumps, hydrogen boilers, and hydrogen blending strategies.
  • Hydrogen Integration Planning: Developed optimisation frameworks for evaluating hydrogen infrastructure deployment and its interaction with renewable energy systems and electricity networks across Great Britain.
  • Game-Theoretic Energy Planning: Applied competitive and cooperative game theory approaches to model strategic investment decisions and infrastructure planning for hydrogen integration under different policy and technology scenarios.
  • Policy Navigation Frameworks: Explored policy-driven pathways and planning strategies for achieving net-zero targets while balancing operational costs, emissions reduction, and system flexibility.
  • Renewable Energy Flexibility: Assessed hydrogen’s role as a flexibility vector for reducing renewable curtailment and supporting high renewable penetration scenarios in future integrated energy systems.

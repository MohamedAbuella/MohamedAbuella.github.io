HI-ACT (Hydrogen Integration for Accelerated Energy Transitions) is a UK research project funded by the Engineering and Physical Sciences Research Council (EPSRC) under Grant No. EP/X038823/2.

In this project, we explored hydrogen integration pathways to support Great Britain’s transition toward a resilient and net-zero multi-vector energy system through optimisation, planning, socio-technical analysis, and game-theoretic modelling across electricity, gas, hydrogen, and heating sectors. The following sections summarise the regional and national research activities, including related blogs, publications, interactive tools, and GitHub repositories developed throughout the HI-ACT project.

Regional Scale: North of Tyne (NoT)

- For exploring residential heating decarbonisation pathways in the North of Tyne region, please refer to our Decarbonizing Residential Heating in the North of Tyne blog.

- For evaluating techno-economic performance of heating technologies, please refer to our Interactive Levelized Cost of Thermal Energy (LCOT) Calculator blog.

- For analysing hydrogen blending strategies for sustainable heating systems, please refer to our Hydrogen Blending for Sustainable Energy blog.

- For socio-technical and hydrogen-driven pathways for residential heating decarbonisation in the North of Tyne, please refer to our IEEE publication.

- For hydrogen-based decarbonisation strategies and energy efficiency analysis in residential heating systems, please refer to our MDPI Energies publication.

- For the simulation framework and modelling environment developed for residential heating decarbonisation studies in the North of Tyne region, please refer to our Residential_Heating_NoT_Simulation GitHub repository.

National Scale: Great Britain (GB)

- For discussing hydrogen supply chain development and lessons from renewable energy integration, please refer to our Future of Hydrogen Supply Chains: Lessons from Solar Energy Integration blog.

- For exploring cooperative game theory applications in energy systems profit allocation, please refer to our Profit Allocation in a Simple Energy System Using Cooperative Game Theory blog.

- For interactive long-term planning and optimisation of future energy assets, please refer to our Interactive Tool for Long-Term Planning of Different Energy Assets blog.

- For game-theoretic modelling of hydrogen integration pathways in Great Britain, please refer to our A Game-Theoretic Framework for Hydrogen Integration to Accelerate Energy Transitions in Great Britain blog.

- For analysing the impact of policy mechanisms such as CO₂ pricing and Contracts for Difference (CfD), please refer to our Interactive Policy Navigator for CO₂ and CfD in Energy Systems blog.

- For game-theoretic optimisation of hydrogen integration pathways in Great Britain, please refer to our IET conference publication.

- For comparative analysis of competitive and cooperative hydrogen infrastructure investment frameworks, please refer to our International Journal of Hydrogen Energy publication.

- For cooperative planning approaches for hydrogen blending in Great Britain’s integrated energy system, please refer to our MDPI Energies publication.

- For policy-navigation frameworks exploring hydrogen integration pathways toward net-zero in Great Britain, please refer to our SSRN preprint.

- For the HI-ACT project resources, presentations, and supporting materials, please refer to our HI-ACT GitHub repository.

- For integrated energy system modelling and optimisation studies for Great Britain, please refer to our GB_IES GitHub repository.

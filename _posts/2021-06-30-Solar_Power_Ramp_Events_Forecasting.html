This blog post wraps up the doctoral research outcomes completed during my PhD studies at the University of North Carolina at Charlotte (UNC Charlotte) from 2014 to 2018, under the supervision of Dr. Badrul Chowdhury.

The rapid expansion of variable renewable energy sources, particularly solar photovoltaic (PV) systems, introduces significant uncertainty and operational challenges into modern electric power grids. To support high-penetration solar integration and enhance grid situational awareness, my PhD research focused on developing data-driven predictive, descriptive, and prescriptive analytics. The following sections summarise my doctoral research activities, organized by core thematic fields, including related publications, master's and doctoral theses, and their official links.

Theme 1: Machine Learning & Ensemble Architectures for Solar Power Point Forecasting

- For deploying off-the-shelf machine learning tools alongside ensemble frameworks to produce robust day-ahead predictions, please refer to our IEEE ISGT 2017 conference publication on Random Forest Ensembles of SVR.

- For a deep assessment of Support Vector Regression configurations and baseline comparative models in deterministic solar forecasting, please refer to our arXiv preprint on Support Vector Regression modeling.

- For analyzing the application of Artificial Neural Networks (ANN) in establishing non-linear curve-fitting mapping structures for solar generation forecasting, please refer to our IEEE NAPS 2015 publication on ANN-based forecasting.

Theme 2: Probabilistic Forecasting & Grid Uncertainty Quantification

- For utilizing Random Forest ensemble learning structures to generate hour-ahead nonparametric probabilistic forecasts that measure baseline grid risks and uncertainties, please refer to our IEEE NAPS 2017 publication on Hourly Probabilistic Forecasting.

- For exploring simple linear statistical setups to formulate a baseline probabilistic forecasting framework for variable solar generation outputs, please refer to our IEEE SoutheastCon 2015 publication on Multiple Linear Regression analysis.

Theme 3: Solar Power Ramp Events Detection and Post-Processing

- For the overarching framework, methodologies, and full scope of our data-driven predictive mitigation strategies, please refer to my full PhD Dissertation: A Post-Processing Approach for Solar Power Combined Forecasts of Ramp Events (UNC Charlotte Dissertation).

- For the development of a data-driven post-processing adjusting approach targeted at resolving high-error forecast anomalies and improving hour-ahead combined forecasts during sharp ramp events, please refer to our Renewable Energy (Elsevier) journal paper.

- For incorporating estimated ramp rates to mitigate systemic forecasting errors and optimize final blended solar generation forecasts, please refer to our IET Renewable Power Generation journal paper.

- For framing solar power ramp detection as a categorical problem through advanced machine learning classification techniques, please refer to our IEEE PEDG 2018 conference publication.

- For evaluating the descriptive qualities and validating combined forecasting methods strictly from a ramp event perspective, please refer to our IEEE PES General Meeting 2018 conference publication.

- For evaluating the implementation of our data-driven adjusting post-processing scheme specifically applied to very short-term solar PV power predictions, please refer to our IEEE Maghreb Meeting 2021 publication.

Theme 4: Optimization & Sensitivity Methods for Wind and Integrated Power Systems

- For the foundational analysis regarding particle swarm optimization layouts applied to baseline power networks embedded with wind generators, please refer to my Master's Thesis: Study of particle swarm for optimal power flow in IEEE benchmark systems including wind power generators (SIUC Thesis).

- For exploring sensitivity analysis approaches to select the most effective control variables when solving optimal power flow problems using particle swarm optimization, please refer to our arXiv preprint on control variables selection.

- For evaluating economic dispatch models designed to seamlessly balance integrated wind power infrastructures using particle swarm optimizations, please refer to our arXiv preprint on integrated wind systems economic dispatch.
